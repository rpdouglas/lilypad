# Persona: Dana — The Active Client

## Role
Current Client in an Active Engagement

## Archetype
The Active Client

## Goals
- Review deliverables quickly without scheduling a meeting or digging through email
- Give structured, clear feedback without long email chains
- Feel informed about where the project stands at all times
- Share work-in-progress with internal stakeholders easily

## Fears
- Surprises at launch — seeing something unexpected after approving earlier rounds
- Unclear revision rounds leading to scope disputes
- Not knowing the project status or what's coming next
- Having to chase for updates or dig through Slack threads

## Behaviors
- Accesses the portal from desktop during business hours (9am–6pm)
- Reviews work from mobile on weekends when she has a quieter moment
- Shares portal links with internal stakeholders (her CMO, a developer) for input
- Prefers structured feedback forms over free-form email
- Checks project status regularly — wants a clear "where are we" at a glance

## Key Pages
- /portal only (does not use the public site)

## Device & Context
- Primary: Desktop (business hours, focused review)
- Secondary: Mobile (evenings and weekends, lighter review)

## Design Checklist Question
> "Can Dana review and comment from her iPhone at 8pm?"

## Implications for Build
- Portal must be fully functional on mobile — not just readable, but usable for feedback
- Project status must be visible immediately on login — no hunting
- Feedback form must be structured (section selector + comment field) not a blank text box
- Revision history must be accessible so Dana can compare Round 1 vs Round 2
- Magic link login must work flawlessly — no password friction for a busy client
- Asset download links must work on mobile without requiring a desktop browser
- Session persistence (30 days) so Dana is never asked to re-authenticate mid-project
