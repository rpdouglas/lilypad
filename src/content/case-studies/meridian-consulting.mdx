## The Brief

Meridian is a boutique change management consultancy. The partners had decades of experience leading transformations at Fortune 500 companies — and a website that looked like it was built from a template in 2019.

When they came to us, their biggest problem wasn't finding clients. It was explaining, in a single clear sentence, why someone should choose Meridian over a larger firm or a solo consultant. They'd been leading with credentials. What they needed to lead with was conviction.

## The Approach

The discovery phase made something clear quickly: Meridian's real differentiator wasn't their process or their pedigree. It was their willingness to stay in the room when things got hard. Bigger firms write the report and leave. Meridian stays through implementation.

That's a brand — if you know how to say it.

We spent the first week building a messaging architecture around that insight. Then we moved to the site. The goal was to show, not tell: case studies built around outcomes, not activities; a homepage that opened with the client's problem before mentioning Meridian once.

Work completed:

- Brand positioning and messaging framework
- Full site redesign — four pages
- Case study templates with a structured Brief → Approach → Outcome format
- Brand voice guide for the partners to write their own future content

## The Result

Six months after launch, Meridian's homepage conversion rate had risen 28%. More meaningfully, the quality of inbound leads changed — prospects arrived knowing what Meridian did and why they wanted it specifically, which compressed the sales cycle and shifted conversations away from price.

Average contract value increased 40% in the first two quarters. The partners attribute some of that to market conditions, and some of it to walking into every pitch with a brand that matched the quality of their work.

> "We went from 'we don't know how to explain what we do' to a homepage that actually converts. The strategy work alone was worth it."

— Jordan M., Co-Founder, Meridian
