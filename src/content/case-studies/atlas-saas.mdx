## The Brief

Atlas came to us three months before their seed round pitch. Their product — a workflow automation tool for ops teams at mid-market companies — was genuinely good. But their website had a 70% bounce rate, investors weren't responding to outreach, and their own team couldn't agree on a single sentence that described what they did.

The problem wasn't the product. It was that nobody outside the founding team could see what we saw when we looked at it.

## The Approach

We started where we always start: not with design, but with positioning.

Over two days, we ran a structured workshop with the Atlas founding team. The goal was to get out of their heads — to stop describing Atlas by its features and start describing it by the outcome it creates for the specific person who needs it most.

What we found: Atlas wasn't a workflow tool. It was the thing that let ops managers stop babysitting their teams and start doing actual strategy. That's a different sentence, aimed at a different emotion.

From there, we rebuilt everything:

- Messaging architecture: one positioning statement, three proof points, a homepage flow built around the ops manager's specific anxieties
- Visual identity: clean, confident, system-like — the visual language of a tool that respects your intelligence
- New site: five pages, built fast and accessibly, with every heading tested against the positioning

The whole thing — strategy to launch — took four weeks.

## The Result

Two weeks after launch, Atlas closed their seed round.

Their bounce rate dropped from 70% to 31%. Investor demo requests came in without cold outreach for the first time. The CEO told us the positioning work alone changed how they presented in every meeting — not just on the website.

> "Lily Pad didn't just build us a site — they gave us a brand we're proud to pitch."

— Alex R., Founder, Atlas
